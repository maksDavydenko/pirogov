OUR PARTNERS

Jilin University (China)

The leading national university in the PRC, under the direct control of the Ministry of Education of the PRC, was founded in 1946.

The university has more than 50 multimedia classrooms and specialized classes for language training. Special attention is paid to the equipment of 20 fundamental science laboratories.

Jilin University has established partnerships with more than 130 universities, colleges and research institutes from 40 countries and districts. The total number of foreign students studying at the university is more than 1.2 thousand.

  • Website of the Jilin University
Jianan University (China)

The university consists of 17 schools and offers 48 undergraduate programs of 10 disciplines, including technology, science, literature, economics, management, law, medicine, agriculture, education and the arts. He has 4 post-doctoral research programs in the field of technology of light industry and mechanical engineering, food science and technology, management of science and technology and the textile industry. The university has the right to issue a master's degree in 24 programs, and doctoral degrees in 5 programs.

  • Website of the Jianan University
University of Humanitas, Rozzano (Italy)

The Italian private university that teaches medicine is located in Rozzano, near the Humanitas Research Hospital. The university provides training in six programs in general medicine in English and three courses in nursing.

The university was founded in 2014.

  • Website of the University of Humanitas
Ryazan State Medical University State Medical University named after academician I.P. Pavlov

One of the largest universities in Ryazan. Founded in 1943 as the Moscow Medical Institute of the Ministry of Health of the RSFSR. In 1950, he was transferred to Ryazan and renamed the Ryazan Medical Institute named after Academician I.P. Pavlov. In 1993, transformed into a university.

  • Website of the University of Ryazan
Yerevan State Medical University Mkhitar Heratsi (Armenia)

YSMU is a higher educational institution where highly qualified medical personnel are trained in all specialties. For 75 years of its existence, the university has passed a rich historical path and plays an indisputable role in the training of medical personnel. Hospitals, clinics and centers that are part of the university, form the Council of University Clinics. The direct management of the work of the University Council, the Rectorate and the Council of Clinics is provided by the Rector of YSMU.

  • Website of the University of Yerevan
University of Tsukuba (Japan)

One of the oldest national universities in Japan, located in the city of Tsukuba, Ibaraki Prefecture, Kanto region. The university has 28 academic departments and schools, with a total of 16,500 students.

Graduates of the University of Tsukuba are the famous Nobel laureates: Leo Esaki, Hideaki Shirakawa, Shin-Ichiro Tomonaga.

Education at the University of Tsukuba is undergraduate and graduate programs. The University includes a research center, a university hospital, and laboratory schools.

  • Website of the University of Tsukuba
University of Maribor (Slovenia)
The second largest university in Maribor, Slovenia, was founded in 1975.

Currently, the university has 17 faculties. It has 16,000 students. The university has branches throughout Slovenia.

Since 1998, the University of Maribor has been participating in the EU Framework for Research and Technology Development.

The University of Maribor considers international activities as one of its main areas and actively participates in various international programs.

  • Website of the University of Maribor
Federal University of Sao Paulo (Brazil)

The university was founded in 1933. Until 2005, he specialized only in the teaching of medicine, but later became multi-disciplinary in the framework of the Brazilian Federal University Reform program.

The University teaches medicine and health courses, nursing, physical therapy and education, physiology, occupational therapy for the mentally ill, history, social sciences, philosophy, pedagogy, chemistry, chemical engineering, history and art, IT technology, business, and many others.

The university has 6 campuses.

  • Website of the Federal University of Sao Paulo
Kazakh National Medical University. S.D. Asfendiyarov (Kazakhstan)
Founded in 1930. In KazNMU . S.D. Asfendiyarova works well-known scientists and teachers of Kazakhstan, academicians of the National Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Kazakhstan, the Russian Academy of Medical Sciences, the Academy of Preventive Medicine of the Republic of Kazakhstan, International Academies, Honored Scientists and Educators, Honored Doctors and Pharmacists.
  • Website of the Kazakh National Medical University
Grodno State Medical University (Belarus)
Grodno State Medical University is a historical successor of the Grodno Medical Academy (1775-1781). On August 9, 1958, by the decision of the Council of Ministers of the BSSR, it was decided to open the Grodno Medical Institute. Initially, there was only medical faculty. Later, in 1970, the preparatory department was opened, in 1979 - the pediatric faculty. In 2000, GrGMI was granted university status.
  • Website of the University of Grodno State Medical University
Bukhara State Medical Institute Abu Ali ibn Sino (Uzbekistan)
The Bukhara State Medical Institute was organized in October 1990 by order of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan. By a decree of the Presidium of the Regional Council of People’s Deputies of the Bukhara Region in March 1991, the institute was named after Abu Ali ibn Sino (Avicenna). One of the leading higher medical educational institutions of Uzbekistan and Central Asia.
  • Website of the University of Bukhara State Medical Institute
Ben-Gurion University, Negev, Beer-Sheva (Israel)

The University was founded in 1969 in the city of Beer-Sheva, and since 1973 it is called “Ben-Gurion” in honour of the Israel’s first Prime minister to contribute the founding of the University.

Today the Ben-Gurion University has more than 14000 teachers and students. There are 4 faculties, management school and several scientific research institutes.

The faculty of medicine started in 1974. The faculty prepares the doctors of various profiles basing on one of the leading hospitals of Israel – the “Soroka medical centre” in Beer-Sheva.

  • Website of the Ben-Gurion University
Vitebsk state Order of People’s Friendship medical university (Belarus)

The University was opened on 1 November 1934 as a hospital-medical university.

Vitebsk state Order of People’s Friendship medical university is one of the biggest higher education institutions in the Republic of Belarus. Nowadays more than 6000 students and listeners are studying in this University.

The University has: the VSMU clinic, the clinic of the Faculty of Dentistry, 2 drugstores, control-analytic laboratory.

The University supports and develops versatile contacts in the sphere of medical education and personnel training, scientific researches and information support with the number of major institutions of Russia, CIS and the foreign countries.

  • Website of the Vitebsk state medical university
Semey medical university (Kazakhstan)

On 6 September 1953 in Semipalatinsk on the basis of the Resolution of the Council of Ministers of the USSR and the Order of the Ministry of health of the USSR the Semipalatinsk medical university was opened.

The University has its own clinical base and the branch in Pavlodar. More than 3900 students from all regions of Kazakhstan, neighbouring and foreign countries study on 8 faculties, and also more than 2000 doctors raise their qualifications.

The University teaches its future specialists in Kazakh, Russian and English in the specialties of: baccalaureate – “Nursing care”, “General medicine”, “Public health”, “Stomatology”, “Pharmacy”; internships; master course – “Public health” and “Medicine”, doctoral PhD – “Medicine”.

  • Website of the Semey medical university
Astana medical university (Kazakhstan)

The decree on the opening of the University was issued on 26 October 1964 by the Council of Ministers of the Kazakh SSR.

In total from 1997 to 2001 on the basis of the University 10 branches of the scientific centres and scientific research institutes were opened. Among them are the branch of the Scientific research institute ща cardiology, the branch of the Tuberculosis scientific research institute and the branch of the Scientific centre of the problems of the healthy lifestyle forming of the Republic of Kazakhstan.

Today the University accepts students for the following educational programs: "General medicine", "Stomatology", "Public health", "Nursing care", "Pharmacy", "Medico-preventive care".

  • Website of the Astana medical university
University of Perugia (Italia)

The University of Perugia is one of the first free universities of Italy. It was founded on 8 September 1308 on the basis of the bulla of the Pope Clement V and currently it is one of the biggest and oldest universities of Italy.

Currently the University has 11 faculties, among which are the faculty of Pharmacy and the faculty of Medicine. The University has approximately 28000 students.

  • Website of the University of Perugia
University of Lisbon (Portugal)

The University of Lisbon is the largest university in Portugal and the fourth largest institution of higher education in the Iberian Peninsula.There are 106 research centers and laboratories at the university, which conduct research in the fields of telecommunications, bio- and nanotechnology, robotics, plasma and nuclear fusion, and molecular medicine.Education at the Faculty of Medicine of the University of Lisbon lasts 6 years (each cycle is 3 years and a total of 360 ECTS credits). At the end of 6 years, a master's degree in medicine is awarded.

  • Website of University of Lisbon
The University of Rome Tor Vergata (Italia)

The University was founded as the second state University of Rome in 1972.

Since 2018 it works in the new complex at 50 Krakow street. Educational activities began in 1982, when the educational process began at the faculties of engineering, law, medicine, natural Sciences and Humanities. The faculty of Economics was founded in 1987.

Currently, the rector of the University is Professor of the faculty of medicine and surgery Renato Lauro.

  • Website of the University of Rome Tor Vergata
Haiphong University Of Medicine and Pharmacy (Vietnam)

Haiphong University of Medicine and Pharmacy is a research and training centre of medicine and pharmacy science, transferring reputable technology domestically and internationally and providing high-quality medical human resources to meet the demand of people healthcare and protection, especially medical major of sea-island.

Haiphong University of Medicine and Pharmacy currently has more than 700 staffs and lecturers, in which 500 permanent staffs in university and 200 staffs in hospitals, including 7 Professors; 28 Associate Professors; 77 PhDs, 180 Masters, 65 specialist II doctors, or equivalent, and 285 concurrent post lecturers at main practice facilities.

The annual number of new entrants to the university is 950 undergraduate and 300 postgraduate students.

  • Website of Haiphong University Of Medicine and Pharmacy
Tashkent Pediatric Medical Institute

Tashkent Pediatric Medical Institute (TashPMI) was opened in 1972. It is the main institution of the republic for the training of pediatric doctors (pediatricians). There are four faculties in the institute: pediatric; general medicine; medical and pedagogical; nurse with higher education. Over the years, more than 25,000 people graduated. Currently, more than 4,000 students study at the institute, including international students. Training of specialists is carried out in 26 areas of the magistracy and 23 areas of clinical residency. Research and educational work is carried out at the clinic at the institute, as well as in scientific and practical centers of Tashkent.
In 2015, a children's heart surgery center was created on the basis of TashPMI. Cardiocenter is the leading in its direction in Central Asia. Surgeons provide cardiac surgery for children with congenital heart defects.

  • Website of Tashkent Pediatric Medical Institute
